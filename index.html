Talk Domains – Scope and Sequence

Domains: Grades:

Grade 1

Exploratory Talk

Uses talk to explore learning and thinking → Thinks aloud during problem solving.

Shares opinions and simple explanations → Uses “I think…” or “Because…”

Participates in partner and small‑group discussions → Turn‑and‑talks, guided group talk.

Asks questions to clarify understanding → “Why?” “How did you know?”

Reflects on self as a speaker and listener (with guidance) → “I listened.” “I spoke louder.”

Presentational Talk

Retells stories or experiences in sequence → Uses “first, then, last.”

Delivers short, rehearsed messages → Practices before sharing.

Uses basic voice control and gestures → Faces audience; uses hands naturally.

Examples of Presentational Talk (POL‑aligned):

Show‑and‑tell

Oral retelling of a story or experience

Sharing favourite books or activities

Simple oral reports about classroom or family experiences

Grade 2

Exploratory Talk

Builds on others’ ideas during discussion → “I agree because…” “That reminds me…”

Uses talk to explain thinking and problem solve → Explains reasoning in math or inquiry.

Asks relevant questions → Questions connect directly to topic.

Participates consistently in group talk → Contributes regularly.

Identifies emerging talk strategies → Notices what helps discussion work.

Presentational Talk

Prepares short oral presentations → Presents learning from reading or inquiry.

Organizes talk with a beginning and ending → Introduces topic; gives conclusion.

Uses visuals to support meaning → Refers to drawings or charts.

Adjusts language slightly for audience and purpose → More formal whole‑class language.

Examples of Presentational Talk (POL‑aligned):

Short oral reports on a topic of interest

Descriptive reports about an event or observation

Book talks

Explaining how something was done (simple procedures)

Grade 3

Exploratory Talk

Explores multiple ideas and viewpoints → Considers different answers in discussion.

Builds, clarifies, and reshapes ideas through talk → Revises thinking after hearing peers.

Explains reasoning with simple justification → Supports opinions with examples.

Responds directly to peers’ ideas → Refers to what others said.

Presentational Talk

Delivers short, structured presentations → Shares prepared information clearly.

Uses subject‑appropriate vocabulary → Science, math, social studies terms.

Begins adjusting register for different contexts → Different language for report vs sharing.

Examples of Presentational Talk (POL‑aligned):

Oral reports based on reading or inquiry

Book talks and chapter summaries

Explaining a process or decision

Presenting findings from research or observation

Grade 4

Exploratory Talk

Sustains discussion to co‑construct meaning → Extends ideas over multiple turns.

Uses talk to plan, reflect, and problem solve → Discusses next steps before acting.

Considers and responds to peers’ viewpoints → Acknowledges different perspectives.

Participates confidently in collaborative talk → Speaks without prompting.

Presentational Talk (Signature Context: Interviewing)

Plans and conducts interviews → Interviews peers, adults, or characters.

Asks purposeful questions → Questions aligned with topic and goal.

Adjusts tone, language, and register → Polite, clear interview language.

Organizes information for oral presentation → Summarizes findings clearly.

Examples of Presentational Talk (POL‑aligned):

Interviews (peer, adult, character‑based)

Oral presentation of interview findings

Inquiry‑based reports

Explanations of how something works

Grade 5

Exploratory Talk

Engages in sustained academic dialogue → Participates in extended inquiry talk.

Explores multiple perspectives → Compares viewpoints.

Uses talk to hypothesize, analyze, and rethink ideas → Revises thinking with evidence.

Reflects metacognitively on learning → Explains how talk supported thinking.

Presentational Talk (Signature Context: Explaining Mathematical and Scientific Thinking)

Provides oral explanations of mathematical reasoning → Explains steps used to solve problems.

Explains strategies and processes clearly → Logical, sequenced explanations.

Uses precise subject‑specific vocabulary → Accurate math and science terms.

Responds to questions to clarify understanding → Rephrases and elaborates.

Examples of Presentational Talk (POL‑aligned):

Oral explanations of math reasoning

Explaining scientific processes or experiments

Inquiry presentations

Structured oral reports based on research

Grade 6

Exploratory Talk

Leads and participates in sustained inquiry discussions → Advances group thinking.

Challenges and refines ideas respectfully → Disagrees constructively.

Adjusts participation role strategically → Leads, contributes, or listens.

Reflects independently on communicative growth → Identifies strengths and needs.

Presentational Talk (Signature Context: Explaining Processes and Reasoning)

Explains complex processes step by step → Clear cause‑and‑effect explanations.

Justifies conclusions using evidence → Refers to data and observations.

Adapts presentation style to the audience → Clarifies or elaborates as needed.

Uses rhetorical and organizational strategies intentionally → Emphasis through pacing and tone.

Self‑evaluates oral communication and sets goals → Reflects and plans improvement.

Examples of Presentational Talk (POL‑aligned):

Oral explanations of scientific and mathematical processes

Persuasive presentations or debates for peers

Inquiry and research presentations

Public service messages or advocacy speeches

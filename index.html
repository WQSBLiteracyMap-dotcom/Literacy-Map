Talk – By Grade

Grade 1 – Exploratory Talk

Uses talk to explore learning and thinking→ Thinks aloud during problem solving.

Shares opinions and simple explanations→ Uses “I think…” or “Because…”

Participates in partner and small‑group discussions→ Turn‑and‑talks, guided group talk.

Asks questions to clarify understanding→ “Why?” “How did you know?”

Reflects on self as a speaker and listener (with guidance)→ “I listened.” “I spoke louder.”

Grade 1 – Presentational Talk

Retells stories or experiences in sequence→ Uses “first, then, last.”

Delivers short, rehearsed messages→ Practices before sharing.

Uses basic voice control and gestures→ Faces audience; uses hands naturally.

Grade 4 – Presentational Talk (Signature Context: Interviewing)

Plans and conducts interviews→ Interviews peers, adults, or characters.

Asks purposeful questions→ Questions aligned with topic and goal.

Adjusts tone, language, and register→ Polite, clear interview language.

Organizes information for oral presentation→ Summarizes findings clearly.

Presentational Talk – All Grades

Grade 1: Retells stories or experiences in sequence → Uses “first, then, last.”

Grade 2: Prepares short oral presentations → Presents learning from reading or inquiry.

Grade 3: Delivers short, structured presentations → Shares prepared information clearly.

Grade 4: Signature Context: Interviewing

Grade 5: Signature Context: Explaining Mathematical and Scientific Thinking

Grade 6: Signature Context: Explaining Processes and Reasoning
